Building pre-verbal Communication

Techniques to help neurodiverse children develop pre-verbal communication skills.

Building pre-verbal Communication

Here is a video by Anjali Barretto, a speech therapist at Sethu, Goa on building pre-verbal communication. Key points she mentions are:

  • Engagement is the building block of communication
  • when your child is having fun the chances that they engage and communicate with you is much higher so play every day
  • remember to follow your child's lead
  • change the way you speak
  • pause or wait and then respond
  • manipulate the home environment to encourage your child to use whatever communication skills they have

Please note that these interventions are shared for general guidance. Designing and executing interventions - more importantly - noticing how the child responds and modifying the interventions so the child does not get distressed, is a specialist job. Consult qualified therapists as necessary.
